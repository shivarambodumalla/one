Not all resume checkers are equal.

Some score your CV and leave you to fix it yourself. Some charge $29/month for features you can get free elsewhere. Some only check formatting and miss the bigger picture entirely.

This is an honest comparison of the best free resume checker tools available in 2026 — what each one does, what it doesn't do, and which one is actually worth your time.

**What a good resume checker should do**

Before comparing tools, let's establish what a resume checker actually needs to do to be useful:

**Score your CV against ATS criteria** — not just formatting, but keyword match, bullet quality, section completeness, and overall readability for automated systems.

**Show you specifically what's wrong** — not a vague "your CV needs improvement" but "you're missing these 8 keywords" and "these 3 bullets are too vague."

**Help you fix it** — the most important step that most tools skip entirely. Showing you the problem without helping you solve it is only half the job.

**Match your CV to specific job descriptions** — because the same CV will score differently for different roles. Generic scoring misses the point.

**2\. Jobscan — Best for keyword matching**

Jobscan is one of the oldest ATS checkers. It pioneered the idea of matching your CV to a job description for a keyword score.

**What it does:**

*   ATS keyword match score
    
*   Missing keyword list
    
*   Basic formatting suggestions
    

**What it doesn't do:**

*   No AI rewriting
    
*   No cover letter generation
    
*   No interview preparation
    
*   No templates
    

**Price:** $29/month. Free tier is very limited — 5 scans total.

**The problem:** Jobscan tells you what's wrong but doesn't fix it. You pay $29/month to get a score and a list of keywords. The rewriting is entirely up to you.

**Verdict:** Good at one thing — keyword matching. Overpriced for what it offers compared to free alternatives.

**3\. Resumeworded — Best for detailed feedback**

Resumeworded provides more detailed line-by-line feedback than Jobscan. Each bullet gets scored individually.

**What it does:**

*   Per-bullet scoring and suggestions
    
*   ATS compatibility check
    
*   LinkedIn profile review
    

**What it doesn't do:**

*   No AI rewriting (suggestions only)
    
*   No job matching
    
*   No cover letter
    
*   No interview prep
    

**Price:** Free tier limited to 3 reviews. Paid from $19/month.

**Verdict:** More granular feedback than Jobscan but still stops short of actually fixing anything. Better value than Jobscan but still outperformed by free alternatives.

**4\. Teal — Best free job tracker with basic CV tools**

Teal started as a job tracker and added CV tools. It's the most complete free job search platform in terms of breadth — but the resume checker is basic.

**What it does:**

*   Resume builder with templates
    
*   Basic ATS scoring
    
*   Job application tracker
    
*   Chrome extension for saving jobs
    

**What it doesn't do:**

*   No AI rewriting
    
*   No strong keyword matching
    
*   No cover letter generation
    
*   No interview preparation
    

**Price:** Free tier available but limited. Paid from $29/month.

**Verdict:** Good if you want an all-in-one job search tracker. Weak as a standalone resume checker.

**5\. Enhancv — Best for visual CVs**

Enhancv is primarily a resume builder with strong templates. The ATS checking is a secondary feature.

**What it does:**

*   Beautiful CV templates
    
*   Basic content suggestions
    
*   LinkedIn import
    

**What it doesn't do:**

*   No strong ATS keyword matching
    
*   No AI rewriting
    
*   No job description matching
    
*   No interview preparation
    

**Price:** Free tier limited. Paid from $24.99/month.

**Verdict:** Great if design is your priority. Weak as an ATS checker. Many of Enhancv's templates are not ATS-friendly despite the tool claiming otherwise.
