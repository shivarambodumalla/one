## What Happens When You Don’t Tailor Your Resume

*   Low ATS score
    
*   Missing keywords
    
*   Poor relevance
    
*   Fewer interview calls
    

## Step-by-Step: How to Tailor Your Resume

### Step 1: Analyze the Job Description

Carefully read the job description and identify:

*   Required skills
    
*   Tools and technologies
    
*   Key responsibilities
    

### Pro Tip:

Look for repeated keywords — they matter most.

### Step 2: Match Keywords Exactly

If the job says:

*   “React.js”
    

Don’t write:

*   “Frontend development”
    

Use the exact term.

### Step 3: Adjust Your Skills Section

Reorder your skills based on relevance.

Put the most important ones first.

### Step 4: Rewrite Your Bullet Points

Focus on relevant experience.

### Example:

Generic:

> Built web applications

Tailored:

> Built React.js applications improving user engagement by 25%

### Step 5: Update Your Resume Summary

Your summary should reflect the role.

### Example:

> Frontend Developer specializing in React.js with 3+ years of experience building scalable applications

### Step 6: Remove Irrelevant Content

Don’t clutter your resume.

Remove:

*   Outdated skills
    
*   Irrelevant roles
    
*   Unrelated projects

Never apply without checking your resume score.
