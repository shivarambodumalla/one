## Resume vs CV: Why This Confusion Matters

Many job seekers use “resume” and “CV” interchangeably.

But recruiters don’t.

Using the wrong format can hurt your chances — especially when applying globally.

## What Is a Resume?

A resume is:

*   Short (1–2 pages)
    
*   Tailored for a specific job
    
*   Focused on relevant experience
    

### Used in:

*   USA
    
*   Canada
    
*   Most corporate roles
    

## What Is a CV?

A CV (Curriculum Vitae) is:

*   Detailed (multiple pages)
    
*   Comprehensive career history
    
*   Includes publications, research, certifications
    

### Used in:

*   Academia
    
*   Research roles
    
*   Europe (in many cases)
    

## Key Differences

| Feature | Resume | CV |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Length | 1–2 pages | Multiple pages |
| Purpose | Job-specific | Full career history |
| Customization | High | Low |
| Usage | Corporate jobs | Academic/research |

## What Recruiters Actually Expect in 2026

For most jobs: 👉 Recruiters expect a **resume, not a CV**

They want:

*   Quick readability
    
*   Relevant experience
    
*   Clear impact
    

## Common Mistakes

*   Sending a long CV for a corporate role
    
*   Not tailoring your resume
    
*   Including irrelevant details
    

## How to Choose the Right Format

### Use Resume if:

*   You’re applying to tech, business, or startup roles
    
*   You want to pass ATS
    

### Use CV if:

*   You’re in academia or research
    
*   You need to show full credentials
