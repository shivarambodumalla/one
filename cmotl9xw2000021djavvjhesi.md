If you're paying $24.95/month for Resume.io, you should know what you're paying for — and what you're missing.

Resume.io is one of the most popular resume builders on the internet. It has a clean editor, plenty of templates, and a brand most people recognise. It also has limitations that hurt your interview rate, especially in 2026 when ATS scoring has become the gatekeeper for most online applications.

This article isn't a hit piece. Resume.io built a solid product. But there's a better, free alternative — and you should know about it before your next subscription renewal.

## What Resume.io Does Well

Credit where it's due:

- **Clean editor.** Drag-and-drop, intuitive, fast.
- **Big template library.** 30+ designs across visual styles.
- **Polished output.** Final PDFs look professional.
- **Strong brand trust.** Millions of users have built resumes with it.

If you just want a pretty PDF and you don't mind paying, Resume.io works.

## Where Resume.io Falls Short in 2026

Here's what most reviews don't tell you.

**1. No real ATS scoring.** Resume.io has a "score" feature, but it scores formatting, not ATS readiness. It doesn't tell you which keywords from a specific job description are missing, or how a real ATS would parse your resume.

**2. The free tier is functionally a trial.** You can build a resume — but you can't download it without a paid plan. Free users get a watermark or .txt-only export.

**3. AI features are paywalled.** AI bullet rewrites, cover letter generation, and tailored content all require Pro.

**4. No job match feature.** You can't paste a job description and see how well your resume matches.

**5. No interview prep.** No story bank, no STAR framework support, no JD analysis.

**6. Pricing creep.** $2.95 for a 7-day "trial" auto-renews at $23.95-29.95/month. Cancellation requires explicitly downgrading.
