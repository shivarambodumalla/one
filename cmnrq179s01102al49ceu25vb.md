If you have applied to dozens of jobs and heard nothing back, it is probably not because you are unqualified. It is because your CV never made it past the first filter. The Reality: ATS Filters Everything Most companies today use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to scan resumes before a recruiter even looks at them. That means:

*   No keywords leads to rejection
    
*   Poor formatting leads to rejection
    
*   Weak bullet points lead to rejection
    
*   Missing sections lead to rejection Up to 75 percent of CVs are rejected automatically before a human ever sees them.
    

### The Problem With Traditional CV Advice You have probably heard things like:

*   Keep it one page
    
*   Use strong action verbs
    
*   Tailor your resume But here is the issue. These are generic tips. ATS systems are not generic. They look for specific signals such as:
    
*   Role specific keywords like SQL, Kubernetes, React
    
*   Measurable results with numbers and impact
    
*   Structured sections such as experience, skills, education
    
*   Clean and parseable formatting Without these, your CV does not stand a chance.
    

### What Actually Works To get interviews, your CV needs to do two things:

1.  Pass the ATS Your CV must match what the system is scanning for.
    
2.  Impress the Recruiter Once it passes, it still needs to read well to a human. Most tools only solve one side of this problem.
